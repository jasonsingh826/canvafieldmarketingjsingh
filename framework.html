Part one · Framework

The Integrated Field
Marketing Framework.

A company-agnostic operating model for B2B field marketing. The methodology that informs every program in the documents that follow — applied here using sample data only.

00 · Premise

Most B2B marketing is operating on the wrong model.

B2B marketing has fundamentally changed over the past fifteen years, but most organisations have not updated their operating model. They are running playbooks built for a world where buyers followed a linear funnel, sales controlled information, and marketing's job was to fill the top.

Over-indexing on demand capture
Most teams pour budget into capturing existing intent and almost nothing into creating new demand.
Marketing & Sales misalignment
Marketing measures MQLs. Sales measures revenue. Neither owns the pipeline in between.
Attribution theatre
Last-touch rewards capture programs and makes creation programs invisible.
One-size-fits-all programs
The same webinar gets sent to a CIO and a marketing coordinator.
01 · Context

Three eras of B2B go-to-market.

The market has moved through three operating models. Most organisations are still working in the second.

2008 — 2015
The lead gen era

Fill the funnel, let Sales sort it out. MQL volume was the metric. Linear funnel. Events as lead capture machines.

2015 — 2021
The ABM era

Target right accounts, align with Sales. Account-based replaced lead-based. Pipeline influence replaced volume. Still fundamentally a capture motion.

2021 — NOW
The creation era

Build the market, then capture it. 70%+ of the journey happens before sales contact. Dark social and peer influence drive decisions.

03 · Two motions

Demand creation × demand capture.

Inspired by Refine Labs. These are not two ends of a spectrum — they are two different jobs with different timelines and different ways of proving impact.

Demand creation

Change what buyers believe. Create future pipeline by shaping how the market thinks.
Executive engagement
Intimate experiences that reposition the brand with senior decision-makers.
Thought leadership
Analyst partnerships and co-authored research that establish category authority.
Customer storytelling
Peer-led events with authentic customer voices.
Category-defining events
Owned events that establish category leadership.

Demand capture

Convert existing intent from buyers already in-market or evaluating.
Account-based marketing
Multi-touch campaigns targeting named accounts.
Industry events & trade shows
Sponsorships with speaking slots and executive meetings.
Partner co-marketing
Joint campaigns with strategic partners.
Customer reference programs
Connecting prospects with customers to accelerate procurement.
04 · Account segmentation

ICP tiering — investment follows opportunity size.

Different tiers warrant fundamentally different field motions. Sample ACV ranges shown — values vary by category and geography.

TIER 1 · STRATEGIC
Largest accounts
Complex committees, multi-year contracts. Sample ACV $250K–$1M+. 1:1 named account plans, executive engagement, full buying-committee activation.
TIER 2 · GROWTH
Fast-scaling mid-enterprise
Sample ACV $50K–$250K. 1:few regional roadshows, vertical campaigns, persona-specific ABM.
TIER 3 · COMMERCIAL
High-velocity mid-market
Sample ACV $10K–$50K. 1:many virtual workshops, scaled digital, champion-led motion.
05 · Persona mapping

The buying committee — five archetypes.

Generic enterprise messaging fails because every member of the buying committee is solving a different problem. Sample roles shown for a typical PLG-to-enterprise SaaS sale.

Capture
Champion
Sample: Brand or function head
Already uses the product. Needs enterprise features and governance to pitch internally.
Creation
Gatekeeper
Sample: CIO or CISO
Has not seen the product as enterprise-grade. Needs security validation.
Both
Business Buyer
Sample: CMO or VP Marketing
Knows the product casually. Needs repositioning to a strategic platform.
Creation
LOB Head
Sample: VP Sales, CHRO
Outsources to agencies. Wants to in-house but does not yet see the product as the answer.
Capture
Rationaliser
Sample: Procurement, CFO
Consolidating SaaS spend. Needs TCO comparison and analyst validation.
The implication. Executive dinners reach gatekeepers and LOB heads (creation). Workshops and demos reach champions and rationalisers (capture). A field portfolio must deliberately cover the full committee.
06 · Operating system

Three mechanisms that make the framework live.

Without these, the framework is a deck. With them, it is a working system.

01
Pipeline council with Sales

Weekly reviews to align field programs with active pipeline, account stage, and persona engagement. Field marketing earns its seat by changing what gets done next week.

02
Product data → field signals

Usage data triggers the right program for the right account at the right awareness stage. PLG signals are the connective tissue.

03
Closed-loop attribution

Self-reported for creation, software-attributed for capture. Each motion measured on its own terms.

07 · Measurement

The single biggest mistake — measuring creation and capture the same way.

Last-touch attribution rewards capture and makes creation invisible. Each motion needs its own scoreboard.

Demand creation metrics
• Brand perception shift among target accounts
• Executive engagement depth and frequency
• Analyst coverage and category positioning
• Self-reported attribution
• Approved vendor list additions
Demand capture metrics
• Pipeline generated and influenced
• Event-to-SQO conversion rate
• Cost-per-opportunity by program type
• Software-attributed pipeline and revenue
• Deal velocity acceleration from field touches
How it connects

Awareness model says where. Two motions say which. ICP says how much. Personas say who. Operating system says how it runs. Measurement says what worked.
