01 · Framework · Company-agnostic

The Integrated
Field Marketing
Framework.

An operating model for B2B field marketing — built across fifteen years of standing the function up from zero at five companies.

00 · Premise

Most B2B marketing organisations are operating on a model designed for a different era.

B2B marketing has fundamentally changed over the past fifteen years, but most organisations have not updated their operating model. They are running demand generation playbooks built for a world where buyers followed a linear funnel, sales controlled information, and marketing's job was to fill the top. That world no longer exists.

Over-indexing on demand capture

Most B2B teams pour budget into capturing existing intent — paid search, trade shows, gated content — while doing almost nothing to create new demand. They are fishing in a shrinking pond.

Marketing and Sales misalignment

Marketing measures MQLs. Sales measures revenue. Neither owns the pipeline in between. Field programs get planned in isolation from deal cycles.

Attribution theatre

Last-touch attribution rewards capture programs and makes creation programs invisible. Marketing cuts the activities that build future pipeline because they cannot prove they worked in 30 days.

One-size-fits-all programs

The same webinar gets sent to a CIO and a marketing coordinator. Different buyers at different stages need fundamentally different programs.

01 · Context

Three eras of B2B go-to-market.

2008–2015 · The lead gen era

Fill the funnel, let Sales sort it out. Marketing existed to generate volume. Success was measured by MQL count.

  • Buyers depended on sales reps for information
  • Linear funnel: awareness → interest → decision
  • Marketing measured by volume (MQLs)
  • Events were lead capture machines

2015–2021 · The ABM era

Target the right accounts, align with Sales. ABM replaced spray-and-pray — but it was still fundamentally a capture motion.

  • Buyers self-educated before talking to sales
  • Account-based replaced lead-based
  • Marketing measured by pipeline influence
  • Events were relationship accelerators

2021–now · The creation era

Build the market, then capture it. The best companies realised most of their TAM is not in-market at any given time.

  • Buyers complete 70%+ of the journey before engaging sales
  • Dark social and peer influence drive decisions
  • Marketing measured by pipeline sourced and revenue
  • Events are category-defining experiences
The thesis

Field marketing is the only function that can operate across both demand creation and demand capture — because it sits at the intersection of Sales, Product Marketing, and the customer.

02 · Buyer awareness

Where accounts sit determines which motion to run.

Adapted from Eugene Schwartz's awareness model. Demand creation moves buyers through the left three stages. Demand capture converts the right two.

Unaware Problem aware Solution aware Product aware Most aware
"We outsource all this — it's not our problem" "This is getting expensive and slow" "We need to bring this in-house" "This product could solve it" "We need to buy this"

03 · Two motions

Demand creation × demand capture.

Inspired by Chris Walker's framework at Refine Labs. These are not two ends of a spectrum — they are two different jobs with different timelines and different ways of proving impact.

Demand creation

Change what buyers believe. Create future pipeline by shaping how the market thinks — before they enter a buying cycle.

  • Executive engagement. Intimate experiences that reposition the brand with senior decision-makers.
  • Thought leadership. Analyst partnerships and co-authored research that establish category authority.
  • Customer storytelling. Peer-led events with authentic customer voices.
  • Category-defining events. Owned events that establish category leadership.

Demand capture

Convert existing intent from buyers already in-market or evaluating.

  • Account-based marketing. Multi-touch campaigns targeting named accounts.
  • Industry events and trade shows. Sponsorships with speaking slots and executive meetings.
  • Partner co-marketing. Joint campaigns with strategic partners.
  • Customer reference programs. Connecting prospects with customers to accelerate procurement.
04 · Account segmentation

ICP tiering — investment level follows opportunity size.

Tier Definition Indicative ACV Field approach
Tier 1 · Strategic Largest accounts, complex committees $250K–$1M+ 1:1 named account plans, executive engagement, full buying committee activation
Tier 2 · Growth Fast-scaling mid-enterprise $50K–$250K 1:few regional roadshows, vertical campaigns, persona-specific ABM
Tier 3 · Commercial High-velocity mid-market $10K–$50K 1:many virtual workshops, scaled digital, champion-led motion
05 · Persona mapping

The buying committee — five archetypes.

Generic enterprise messaging fails because every member of the buying committee is solving a different problem. Programs must be designed around the persona, not the title.

Motion Persona Typical title What they're actually solving for
Capture Champion Brand / Creative Director Already uses the product. Needs enterprise features and governance to pitch internally.
Creation Gatekeeper CIO / CISO Has not seen the product as enterprise-grade. Needs security validation.
Creation → Capture Business Buyer CMO Knows the product as a consumer tool. Needs enterprise repositioning.
Creation LOB Head VP Sales / CHRO / VP Comms Outsources to agencies. Wants to in-house but does not yet see the product as the answer.
Capture Rationaliser Procurement / CFO Consolidating SaaS spend. Needs TCO comparison and analyst validation.

Executive dinners reach gatekeepers and line-of-business heads (creation). Workshops and demos reach champions and rationalisers (capture). A field portfolio must deliberately cover the full committee.

06 · Operating system

Three mechanisms that connect the framework into a live system.

Pipeline council with Sales

Weekly reviews to align field programs with active pipeline, account stage, and persona engagement. The forum where field marketing earns its seat — not by reporting on activity, but by changing what gets done next week.

Product data → field signals

Usage data triggers the right field program for the right account at the right awareness stage. PLG signals are the connective tissue that lets field marketing operate at velocity.

Closed-loop attribution

Self-reported for creation, software-attributed for capture. Each motion measured on its own terms — not collapsed into a single number that hides the work that builds future pipeline.

07 · Measurement

The single biggest mistake — measuring creation and capture the same way.

Demand creation measurement

  • Brand perception shift among target accounts
  • Executive engagement depth and frequency
  • Analyst coverage and category positioning
  • Self-reported attribution
  • Approved vendor list additions (long-cycle)

Demand capture measurement

  • Pipeline generated and influenced
  • Event-to-SQO conversion rate
  • Cost-per-opportunity by program type
  • Software-attributed pipeline and revenue
  • Deal velocity acceleration from field touches
How the framework connects

The buyer awareness model tells you where an account sits. Demand creation and capture tell you which motion to run. ICP tiers determine investment level. Persona mapping determines which programs reach which members of the buying committee. Product data is the connective tissue. The measurement system ensures each motion is evaluated on its own terms.
