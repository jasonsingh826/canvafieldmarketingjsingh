Canva B2B Field
Marketing Strategy.

The full strategy. The opportunity, the gaps, the operational challenges, the missing motions, the growth pillars that allocate the budget against them, and a deep-dive on the flagship program.

01 · The opportunity
Canva has won the consumer game. The enterprise game is the next chapter.

Canva has achieved what most enterprise companies spend years trying to build: ubiquitous product adoption. With 265M+ monthly active users and 95% Fortune 500 penetration, the consumer flywheel is proven. The challenge now is converting bottom-up adoption into top-down enterprise contracts — and field marketing is the engine that makes that conversion happen.

Recent moves confirm the enterprise bet: the EY-Canva Alliance for agentic AI in sales workflows, the all-new free Affinity unifying Designer, Photo, and Publisher into a single app — expanding Canva's reach into professional creative workflows, the Canva Design Model as a proprietary foundation for visual AI, and the Creative OS positioning. The narrative is shifting from "easy design" to "AI-powered visual communication platform at enterprise scale." Field marketing has to carry that narrative into every enterprise conversation.

02 · The diagnosis
Five strategic gaps field marketing must close.

Before talking programs, it is worth being honest about where the friction sits. Five gaps stand out — they are the ones that current field activity does not reliably solve for.

GAP 01
The PLG-to-enterprise chasm. Canva has world-class adoption, but converting that adoption into full enterprise deployments — the kind that involve IT, procurement, security review, and replacement of incumbent tools — remains the hardest part of the motion.
GAP 02
Persona-specific value translation. Each member of the buying committee needs to understand specifically what Canva changes for them — not just what the platform does. A CMO needs a different story than a CHRO, who needs a different story than a CIO. Generic enterprise messaging does not move buying committees.
GAP 03
The offboarding question. Most enterprise accounts already have an incumbent stack — Creative Cloud, agency relationships, in-house design teams, brand governance tools. The conversation is not "why Canva?" It is "how do we transition off what we have without disrupting workflows?" Field marketing must help Sales answer that question.
GAP 04
Visual experiences for a visual company. Canva's brand is its competitive moat. Field marketing is the most direct opportunity to make the enterprise motion as unmistakable as the product — immersive, design-forward, distinctive at every touchpoint.
GAP 05
Enterprise has the room to define its own identity. Education and Consumer have established narratives. Enterprise is the chapter where Canva can stand up its own — distinct from both, with its own community, its own customer voice, and its own visible momentum. Field marketing is the function that makes that identity legible to the market.
03 · Field marketing challenges
Four operational challenges — and how I'd address each.

Section 02 describes what is broken in Canva's enterprise field motion specifically. The challenges below are the broader market and operational realities that any field marketing leader at Canva will run into — and the moves required to neutralise them.

CHALLENGE 01
The AI noise problem

Every marketing tool now claims to be AI-powered. Enterprise leaders cannot tell platforms apart and the result is decision paralysis — they wait, or they default to the safe option.

Fix. Be brutally tight on the problem we are addressing, for whom, and how. Every field program leads with the persona-specific problem. Cut through by being narrower than competitors, not louder.
CHALLENGE 02
Switching costs — literal and figurative

Two layers. Literal: contract overlap, agency retainers, training time, migration risk. Figurative: political capital to displace an incumbent, muscle memory of teams, fear of a bad rollout.

Fix. Address the literal cost head-on with the Offboarding Playbook (Section 05). Partner-led discovery workshops and pilots to lower figurative risk. SPIFFs for workshops that turn into commercial offers.
CHALLENGE 03
Demand-capture handoffs

Pipeline does not close in a single function. It moves from marketing-sourced lead to Sales-qualified opportunity to partner-led implementation. Every undocumented handoff is where deals stall.

Fix. Build openly with Sales and Partner teams from day one. Co-design the motion with all three function leads. Sales and partner enablement materials by persona, by play, by stage. Shared definition of "ready for handoff."
CHALLENGE 04
TAM is too large to win without discipline

The largest enterprise upsell opportunity in the company's history sits across Commercial, Government, and Higher Ed. The temptation is to spread investment evenly. The discipline is to compound where it pays back.

Fix. Three focused motions: speed-to-market deals where AI 2.0 is the trigger; highly profitable Strategic and Growth tier accounts; specific acquisition versus expansion motions per persona — same playbook does not work for both.

05 · The offboarding playbook
Canva's missing motion.

The biggest gap in Canva's enterprise field strategy is content and programs that help buyers understand how to transition to Canva, not just adopt it. This is a new pillar of work — five components.

01
Competitive displacement battle cards

Adobe Creative Cloud, Figma Slides, agency retainer contracts, PowerPoint at scale. Honest comparisons Sales can use in active deals.

02
Migration playbooks by persona & stack

How does a Marketing org transition from Adobe? How does HR move comms in-house? How does Sales replace agency-built decks? Each playbook anchored by a customer story.

03
Partner co-marketing for stack integration

Joint events with Salesforce, Microsoft, Workday on integrating Canva into existing workflows. The message: Canva does not replace your platform of record — it enhances it.

04
Customer reference program for offboarding

Connect prospects with customers who have made the same transition. Make the path visible. Powered by the Reference Engine in Section 09.

05
Procurement-ready content pack

TCO calculators, migration cost estimates, security checklists, SOC 2 reports. Everything Procurement needs to greenlight a deal in one place.

A meaningful step-change in deal velocity.

Removing the "but how?" friction — the question that currently stalls bridge-stage deals — is what compresses the cycle. The bigger the offboarding lift in an account, the bigger the unlock when it's removed.

06 · Visual experiences
Canva's brand is its competitive moat. Field is where the moat is most underused.

Design is what makes Canva's product unmistakable. Field programs are the most direct opportunity to make Canva's enterprise motion unmistakable too — immersive, design-forward, distinctive at every touchpoint. Below: four programs that turn the brand asset into a field asset.

Canva Studios at tentpole events

Live design experiences in place of standard booths — celebrity designers, on-the-spot rebranding for attendees, AI-assisted creation demos. The booth becomes the marketing moment.

Immersive executive experiences

Private gallery openings, design studio tours, sessions with Canva's in-house creative team. Memorable encounters with the brand instead of hospitality dinners.

Canva Create for Enterprise

Dedicated enterprise track at Canva Create — or a separate annual Enterprise Summit. Customer-led content, deep product roadmap, immersive brand.

Visual thought leadership

Reports like "State of Visual Communication" delivered as visual artefacts, not PDFs. Interactive microsites, designed publications, video-first storytelling. The medium reinforces the message.

09 · Flagship deep-dive 20% of budget

Create Raving Fans.

Make every B2B customer an organised advocate. Make customer advocacy the primary demand engine for enterprise.

Most B2B reference programs are defensive — "we need a reference for this deal." That treats customers as a fulfilment resource. Canva has something most B2B companies do not: 95% of Fortune 500 are customers, 28M+ paying subscribers, and ~35% YoY revenue growth — an installed base that already loves the product. The asset is unprecedented; the program design needs to match the scale of the asset. The 20% budget allocation in Section 08 funds the six components below.

COMPONENT 01
Pioneer Program

12–15 strategic customers selected annually. Become Canva's design partners — not just customers.

Get: Roadmap access, exec sponsorship, custom AI 2.0 use cases, annual Pioneer Summit.
COMPONENT 02
Champion Network

200+ champions across the customer base. Brand Architects and Force Multipliers identified, organised, activated.

Get: Quarterly roundtables, annual Summit, recognition program, exclusive community.
COMPONENT 03
Peer-to-peer prospecting

Operationalised customer-to-prospect intros. Sub-24-hour matching. Customer "open office hours" for prospects.

Compensation: Recognition and access — never money. Currency is status.
COMPONENT 04
Reference Engine

Software-driven matching. Every customer indexed by persona, vertical, use case, deployment stage, language.

SLA: Sub-24-hour reference. The standard becomes a service-level commitment.
COMPONENT 05
Storytelling at scale

100+ customer stories per year, indexed by persona × industry × use case. Three formats per story.

Formats: 30-sec video (ABM), 90-sec video (events), long-form case study (procurement).
COMPONENT 06
Canva Customer Awards

Annual program. Five awards mapped one-to-one to the personas. Announced at Canva Create Enterprise.

Effect: Aspiration in the customer base. Social proof manufactured at scale.
Operating mechanics
Leadership: Dedicated Customer Marketing leader, reporting to Field Marketing.
Team: 4–5 people — Pioneer, Champion Network, Reference Ops, Storytelling, Events.
Sales rhythm: Weekly review of which references closed which deals.
Tech: ReferenceEdge or Influitive integrated with Salesforce.
KPIs: References-per-month delivered · Reference-to-close rate · Peer-call deal acceleration · NPS lift among enterprise base · Expansion revenue attributable to advocacy.
The thesis. Most companies treat customer reference as a fulfilment function. Canva should treat it as the primary demand engine for enterprise — because the asset already exists. Every Canva customer is already a fan. The only thing missing is the program design that matches the scale of the asset.
Closing

The Canva AI 2.0 moment is the kind of inflection point where field marketing either accelerates the enterprise motion — or misses it entirely. The plan above is built to accelerate.
