03 · Frameworks & methodology
The thinking that informs the framework.

Foundational works the framework, awareness model, persona system, and motion design draw from. Most are widely cited in B2B marketing literature; included here so the methodology is traceable.

Eugene Schwartz
Breakthrough Advertising (1966)

The original five-stage buyer awareness model — Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware. Underpins the awareness strip in the Framework and Strategy pages.

Chris Walker · Refine Labs
Demand creation × demand capture framework

The category-defining shift away from MQL-driven marketing toward separated creation and capture motions. Underpins the Two Motions section in the Framework.

Geoffrey Moore
Crossing the Chasm (1991, revised 2014)

The seminal work on transitions between buyer segments. Informs the "PLG-to-Enterprise chasm" diagnosis in the Strategy page — the gap that field marketing must close at Canva.

April Dunford
Obviously Awesome (2019) and Sales Pitch (2023)

Positioning as a function of competitive alternatives, not features. Underpins the structural rights-to-win and persona-translation methodology in the Messaging page.

Forrester Consulting
Total Economic Impact™ methodology

Framework for evaluating ROI of enterprise technology investments through composite organizations and risk-adjusted modeling. The methodology behind the 414% Canva Enterprise number cited in the deck.

Bessemer Venture Partners
State of the Cloud reports, 2018–2025

Industry benchmarks for SaaS NDR, retention, and pricing. Used as reference for the >120% NDR best-in-class benchmark referenced in the Plan and Strategy.

Gartner CSO research
B2B buying committee composition (multiple reports)

The 6–10 stakeholder committee finding. Underpins the five-persona model in the Messaging and Plan pages — the principle that generic enterprise messaging fails because committees are heterogeneous.

Les Binet & Peter Field
LinkedIn B2B Institute — The B2B Effectiveness Code (2021)

The 95–5 rule (only 5% of buyers are in-market at any time). Underpins the case for demand creation as the larger long-term lever — and for tentpole executive engagement programs in the growth pillars.
