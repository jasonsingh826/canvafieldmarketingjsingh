Canva B2B Messaging
& Positioning.

A platform proposition, six structural rights to win, and the five buying-committee personas — the system that lets every field program speak with one voice and to the right person at the right time.

01 · Platform proposition

One statement, persona-agnostic.

The single positioning everything else translates from. Every persona-specific message is a translation of this proposition into their language.

The proposition
Canva AI 2.0 is the world's only AI platform purpose-built for visual work at enterprise scale.
Powered by the Canva Design Model, one brief becomes a complete, on-brand creative operation across every format and channel. 265 million people already know how to use it.

The proposition lands four claims at once: a category (visual work at enterprise scale), a technical moat (the Canva Design Model), an outcome (a complete creative operation), and an unfair advantage (an installed base of 265M users).

03 · Personas

The five archetypes — situations, not titles.

A title is a container. The persona is the situation, the mandate, the fear driving them, and the version of success they are trying to reach. Each maps to one of the five framework archetypes (Champion, Gatekeeper, Business Buyer, LOB Head, Rationaliser).

PERSONA 01 The Force Multiplier "Build me a content engine without new headcount."

Situation. Told to produce 5–10× the content with the same team. New headcount is not coming. The answer from above is "use AI." Looking for a production system that multiplies output without sacrificing quality.

Value proposition. Multiply your team's output without growing the team. One brief becomes a complete, on-brand campaign across every format and channel.

Rights to win. Design-native AI · Brand Intelligence · 265M users · Connectors

Proof. 7,800+ design hours saved annually. Tyla Group: 21,000 hours in six months. Expedia: 230 hours on a single rebrand. 60% reduction in cost per design.

Framework archetype: LOB Head · creation motion
PERSONA 02 The Brand Architect "Get me brand control back without becoming the bottleneck."

Situation. The brand is being violated thousands of times a week — sales decks off-template, regional teams improvising, social posts the brand team never sees. Wants governance infrastructure, not a gatekeeping role.

Value proposition. Brand consistency by default. Templates, approvals, and brand intelligence that prevent off-brand work without slowing the team down.

Rights to win. Brand Intelligence · Design-native AI · Canva Shield

Proof. FedEx: 77% reduction in brand review requests within three months. 16,000 employees on a unified brand platform. Docusign: opened the brand to the entire company without losing control.

Framework archetype: Champion · capture motion
PERSONA 03 The Mandate Carrier "Make this org AI-native and give me a win I can point to."

Situation. Did not choose this. The board, CEO, or CMO handed them an AI mandate and a timeline. Needs a visible, defensible win — fast — that proves the AI investment is working. Looking for the program that becomes the case study, not the experiment that becomes a footnote.

Value proposition. The fastest, lowest-risk path to AI-native productivity at scale. 265M users means deployment is an unlock, not a project.

Rights to win. 265M users · Connectors · Design-native AI

Proof. 3rd most-used AI tool globally. 27B+ AI uses on Canva. 95% F500 adoption. eXp Realty: rolled out to 82,000 agents — first company-wide Enterprise rollout in real estate.

Framework archetype: Business Buyer · creation → capture motion
PERSONA 04 The Risk Closer "Get control of the AI tools people are already using."

Situation. Employees are using ungoverned AI tools. Data is going places they cannot see. 82% of marketers bypass IT on creative tooling. Not against AI — against the version of AI that creates a breach, a compliance problem, or a legal exposure.

Value proposition. A governed, IT-managed replacement for fragmented creative shadow IT. Same productivity, full visibility.

Rights to win. Canva Shield · Owned AI · 265M users

Proof. Proprietary models 7× faster and 30× cheaper. FedEx SSO rollout completed in minutes. Enterprise admin controls. AI indemnification at no additional cost.

Framework archetype: Gatekeeper · creation motion
PERSONA 05 The Commercial Pragmatist "Cut this spend and make the decision defensible."

Situation. Must justify every line item. The creative stack is fragmented and invisible — Adobe seats, agency retainers, freelancer invoices, point tools. The Adobe stack is breaking under price pressure. Needs a consolidation story procurement can defend.

Value proposition. Consolidate fragmented creative spend into one predictable contract. Up to 60% savings versus Adobe replacement.

Rights to win. Owned AI · Canva Shield · 265M users

Proof. Forrester TEI: 414% ROI. Up to 60% Adobe replacement savings. 28M+ paying subscribers globally and 35% YoY revenue growth — expansion engine working.

Framework archetype: Rationaliser · capture motion

05 · Compelling events & triggers

When buyers come to market — and what triggers the conversation.

Enterprise deals do not close because the buyer woke up and decided they needed visual AI. They close because something in the business changed. These are the five compelling events Canva's enterprise deals consistently turn on — what each one looks like, what signals it, the personas it activates, and how field marketing meets the moment.

COMPELLING EVENT 01 Vendor consolidation & creative SaaS rationalization
The setup

A CFO-driven rationalization exercise. Triggered by a budget cut, an M&A integration, or an "every-vendor-must-justify-itself" mandate. The most common displacement target is Adobe Creative Cloud — non-design teams forced into expensive seats they barely use, plus designers themselves now have a credible alternative since the Affinity acquisition gave Canva professional-grade vector, photo, and layout tools at no additional cost.

Triggers
  • CFO transition or "reduce SaaS by X%" mandate
  • Annual budget review with cut targets
  • M&A activity forcing stack consolidation
  • Incumbent creative-suite renewal within 90 days
  • "We have nine different visual content tools" audit finding
Personas activated
Primary: Commercial Pragmatist
Secondary: Risk Closer
Field response

Procurement-ready TCO calculator and Forrester TEI report front and centre. CFO peer reference. Affinity-positioned displacement battle card to Sales. Joint EBC pairing Procurement and Marketing in the same room.

COMPELLING EVENT 02 AI mandate moving from pilot to operational
The setup

The 2024 question was "are we doing AI?" The 2026 question is "are we doing it at scale, with governance, with a defensible production rollout?" The Mandate Carrier has a board commitment to deliver AI-native productivity within a defined window. They are not running another pilot — they are looking for the program that becomes the case study, not the experiment that becomes a footnote.

Triggers
  • Board-mandated AI program with delivery deadline
  • New CEO or CMO hired to drive transformation
  • IPO preparation or major investor moment
  • "Scale efficiency" or productivity-per-employee mandate
  • Fortune 500 peer announces a company-wide AI rollout
Personas activated
Primary: Mandate Carrier
Secondary: Force Multiplier
Field response

Executive briefing led by Canva CMO. Peer reference call with a Mandate Carrier who has shipped at scale (eXp Realty 82K-agent rollout). Canva Create Enterprise track invitation. Joint POV with the EY-Canva Alliance on agentic AI in marketing workflows.

COMPELLING EVENT 03 Shadow AI & ungoverned tool sprawl
The setup

The CISO or CIO has just learned how many AI tools the marketing team is actually using. Free ChatGPT accounts, Midjourney, image generators, point AI tools — none of them in the SSO directory, none in the audit log, none indemnified. The Risk Closer suddenly has visibility, and a deadline to do something about it.

Triggers
  • Annual security review or SOC 2 / ISO 27001 audit
  • Shadow-IT or AI tool sprawl scan results
  • Data breach at a competitor or peer company
  • New CISO mandate to lock down generative AI
  • Sector regulator activity (financial services, healthcare)
Personas activated
Primary: Risk Closer
Secondary: Commercial Pragmatist
Field response

Canva Shield deep-dive with security architecture diagrams. AI indemnification documentation. Procurement-ready security bundle (SOC 2, ISO 27001, SSO, SAML). Joint session pairing the Risk Closer with the Champion who can advocate internally.

COMPELLING EVENT 04 PLG-to-Enterprise upgrade trigger
The setup

The org has been on Canva for years. A team started with free, more teams added Pro, the marketing org moved to Canva Business — and now there are fifty, a hundred, or three hundred seats sprawled across four billing accounts with no SSO, no central brand controls, no procurement visibility. Someone — usually the original champion — finally raises the question: shouldn't this be one Enterprise contract?

Triggers
  • 50+ Canva Pro or Canva Business seats across the org
  • Multiple billing accounts in different cost centres
  • New SSO or SAML mandate from IT
  • Brand team frustrated by inconsistent output across teams
  • Procurement consolidating SaaS and discovering Canva sprawl
Personas activated
Primary: Force Multiplier
Secondary: Brand Architect (Pragmatist closes)
Field response

PLG-signal-triggered ABM. Champion enablement to build the Enterprise business case internally. Brand Architect briefing on governance unlocks. Adoption-data report — "you're already paying for X seats, here's what Enterprise unlocks." Procurement TCO comparison.

COMPELLING EVENT 05 Brand fragmentation at scale
The setup

The brand is fragmenting in real time. M&A integration adding new entities with their own visual identity. Global expansion launching new regional teams improvising. Franchisee or distributed-team networks producing off-brand collateral at volume. The Brand Architect has visibility into the chaos and a deadline — usually a CMO commitment to "unify the brand by Q4" — to fix it.

Triggers
  • M&A integration adding sub-brands or acquired entities
  • International expansion launching new regional teams
  • Franchise or partner network with brand-quality issues
  • New CMO with a "unify the brand" mandate
  • Brand audit revealing widespread off-template usage
Personas activated
Primary: Brand Architect
Secondary: Force Multiplier
Field response

Brand Kits 2.0 demo with M&A use case. Customer reference from a peer Brand Architect who has unified post-acquisition (FedEx, Docusign). Brand Intelligence walkthrough showing automatic on-brand defaults at scale. Workshop on rolling out brand controls without slowing teams.

How field uses this. Compelling events drive the campaign calendar — not internal product cycles. When intel surfaces a trigger in a target account, the matching field play activates within seven days. This is what "sales-aligned" actually means in practice: the program library is indexed by event, not by quarter.
