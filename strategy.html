Strategy.

The full strategy — diagnosis, programs, and growth pillars. Written for the Field Marketing Lead conversation, May 2026.

01 · The opportunity

Canva has won what most enterprise companies spend years trying to build — ubiquitous product adoption.

With 260M+ monthly active users and 95% Fortune 500 penetration, the consumer flywheel is proven. The challenge now is converting bottom-up adoption into top-down enterprise contracts — and field marketing is the engine that makes that conversion happen.

Recent moves confirm the enterprise bet: the EY-Canva Alliance for agentic AI in sales workflows, the launch of Canva Business bridging Pro and Enterprise tiers, and the Creative OS positioning with the Canva Design Model. The enterprise narrative is shifting from "easy design" to "AI-powered visual communication platform."

But the gap between bottom-up adoption and full enterprise deployment is real. Most accounts that already use Canva at the team level have not taken the step to enterprise — because that conversation is not about features. It is about replacing entrenched workflows, technology contracts, and agency relationships. Field marketing's job is to bridge that gap.

02 · Five strategic gaps

Five gaps field marketing must address.

Before talking about programs, it is worth being honest about where the friction sits. From conversations with the team and observation of the market, five gaps stand out.

01 · The PLG-to-enterprise chasm

Canva has world-class adoption, but converting that adoption into full enterprise deployments — the kind that involve IT, procurement, security review, and replacement of incumbent tools — remains the hardest part of the motion.

02 · Persona-specific value translation

Each member of the buying committee needs to understand specifically what Canva changes for them — not just what the platform does. A CMO needs a different story than a CHRO, who needs a different story than a CIO. Generic enterprise messaging does not move buying committees.

03 · The offboarding question

Most enterprise accounts already have an incumbent stack — Adobe Creative Cloud, agency relationships, in-house design teams, brand governance tools. The conversation is not "why Canva?" It is "how do we transition off what we have without disrupting workflows?" Field marketing must help Sales answer that question.

04 · Visual experiences for a visual company

Canva is a creative brand. Enterprise field programs should look and feel like Canva — immersive, design-forward, visually distinctive. Standard CXO dinners and beige conference booths do not serve the brand or the buyer.

05 · Enterprise's identity problem

Inside Canva, Enterprise sits in the shadow of Education and Consumer. That is a legitimate strategic priority issue — but field marketing has a role to play in establishing enterprise as its own distinct identity, with its own narrative, its own community, and its own visible momentum.

03 · Field marketing challenges

Four challenges to design around — and how I'd address each.

The strategic gaps in Section 02 describe what is broken in Canva's enterprise field motion specifically. The challenges below are different — they are the broader market and operational realities that any field marketing leader at Canva will run into, and the moves required to neutralise them.

01 · The AI noise problem

Every marketing tool now claims to be AI-powered. Every design platform, every workflow tool, every "creative OS" is shouting the same message at the same buyers. Enterprise leaders are telling analysts they cannot tell the platforms apart — and the result is decision paralysis. They wait. Or they default to the safe option. Neither is good for Canva.

Fix. Be brutally tight on the problem we are addressing, for whom, and how. Every field program leads with the persona-specific problem from Section 05 — no "AI for everything" messaging. Specific use cases. Specific outcomes. Specific personas. Cut through the noise by being narrower than competitors, not louder than them.

02 · Switching costs are high — literally and figuratively

The switching cost problem has two layers. The literal cost: contract overlap with Adobe, agency retainers, training time, migration risk. The figurative cost: internal political capital required to displace an incumbent, the muscle memory of teams who know the existing tools, the fear of the rollout going badly on the new leader's watch. Both have to be addressed in parallel, not sequentially.

Fix. Three moves working together:

  • Address the literal cost head-on with content — the Offboarding Playbook (Section 06) is the answer.
  • Partner-led discovery workshops and pilots — let prospects test Canva inside their existing stack, with a partner co-leading, before committing. Lowers the figurative risk because the partner shares accountability for the rollout.
  • SPIFFs for workshops that convert — incentivise the channel to deliver workshops that turn into commercial offers, not demos that disappear into nowhere.

03 · Demand capture handoffs are friction-prone

Pipeline does not close in a single function. It moves from marketing-sourced lead, to Sales qualified opportunity, to a partner-led implementation. Every handoff is a place a deal can stall — and most companies have informal, undocumented handoffs that get worse as the team scales. Canva's enterprise motion will run through Sales, Partner, and Customer Success in parallel. The handoffs need to be operationalised, not improvised.

Fix. Build openly with Sales and Partner teams from day one.

  • Co-design the marketing → sales → partner motion with all three function leads in the room.
  • Sales enablement materials for every persona, every play, every deal stage — so the rep does not have to invent the conversation each time.
  • Partner enablement materials in parallel — so the partner is not selling a different version of Canva than Sales is.
  • A shared definition of what "ready for handoff" means at each step, with the data that needs to travel with it.

04 · The TAM is too large to win without discipline

$5B+ enterprise upsell potential across Commercial, Government, and Higher Education. 100,000+ organisations with 25+ Canva users. The temptation is to spread investment evenly across all of it. The discipline is to pick the highest-velocity, highest-margin segments and run programs that compound there.

Fix. Three focused motions:

  • Speed-to-market deals. Accounts where AI 2.0 is the trigger and procurement readiness is high. Get them in fast, before the window closes.
  • Highly profitable deals. Strategic and Growth tier accounts (Section 10) where ACV justifies the field investment. Resist the pull to go after volume that does not pay back.
  • Specific acquisition and growth motions per persona. Different programs for new logos versus expansion in existing accounts. The same playbook does not work for both.

The bridge stage is where Canva loses deals today. Buyers know Canva works at the team level. They do not know how it replaces their existing stack at the enterprise level. That is a content, partner, and field activation gap — not a product gap.

Most PLG companies over-index on demand capture because product usage naturally generates intent signals. But $100K–$1M enterprise deals require demand creation — the CIO does not know she needs an enterprise visual communication platform. Field marketing is how you make her realise she does.
05 · Buying committee

What each persona needs to hear.

Generic enterprise messaging fails. Each persona needs a specific value translation.

Champion — Brand / Creative Director (Capture)

What they need to hear. Canva Enterprise gives you brand governance at scale. Templates, approval workflows, and team permissions that prevent off-brand work without slowing down the team. You stop being the bottleneck.

What they're already thinking. "I love Canva for my team but I need controls before I can pitch this to leadership."

Field program. Brand Governance roundtable series. Live demos in Canva Studios.

Gatekeeper — CIO / CISO (Creation)

What they need to hear. Canva is enterprise-grade. SOC 2, SSO, SAML, SCIM, audit logs, data residency. Used by 95% of Fortune 500. Cleaner attack surface than the patchwork of design tools and free apps your employees are already using.

What they're already thinking. "I have never evaluated Canva because I assumed it was a consumer tool."

Field program. Security-first executive briefings. Forrester TEI report. EY-Canva Alliance credibility.

Business Buyer — CMO (Creation → Capture)

What they need to hear. Canva is your Creative OS. One platform that connects brand, content velocity, and AI-assisted production — replacing Adobe seats, agency retainers, and standalone tools. ROI is real and Forrester-validated.

What they're already thinking. "My team uses Canva but I see it as the tool, not the platform."

Field program. CMO summits. Customer-led roundtables featuring peer CMOs from FedEx, Docusign, Salesforce.

Line of Business Head — VP Sales / CHRO / VP Comms (Creation)

What they need to hear. You are spending six figures on agencies for decks, internal comms, recruitment collateral, and proposal design. Bring it in-house with Canva and your team produces it faster, on-brand, with AI assistance. Your agency budget becomes headcount.

What they're already thinking. "I outsource this stuff because we don't have a designer — but the costs are getting absurd."

Field program. Function-specific events — Sales Enablement Summit, HR Comms Forum. Use-case demonstrations rather than feature demos.

Financial Decision-Maker — CFO (Capture)

What they need to hear. Canva consolidates fragmented creative spend — Adobe seats, agency retainers, freelancer invoices, point solution subscriptions — into a single contract with predictable enterprise pricing. Forrester TEI shows three-year ROI through reduced agency dependency, faster content velocity, and SaaS consolidation. AI-assisted production means the same headcount produces materially more output.

What they're already thinking. "We are cutting SaaS spend across the board. Why is creative the exception? And what's the actual ROI on this versus what we already have?"

Field program. CFO-specific executive briefings. ROI-led customer references. Forrester TEI report. Joint sessions with CFO peer networks (Gartner CFO Symposium, Argyle).

Rationaliser — Procurement (Capture)

What they need to hear. Replacing your existing creative stack with Canva Enterprise consolidates SaaS spend, reduces agency invoices, and provides a single contract with usage-based pricing. Migration is supported with playbooks and customer references.

What they're already thinking. "How does this consolidate vendors and what's the migration cost?"

Field program. TCO calculators. Procurement-friendly content packs. Migration playbooks.

06 · Offboarding playbook

Canva's missing motion.

The biggest gap in Canva's enterprise field strategy is content and programs that help buyers understand how to transition to Canva, not just adopt it. This is a new pillar of work.

Competitive displacement battle cards

Adobe Creative Cloud, Figma Slides, agency retainer contracts, Microsoft PowerPoint at scale. Honest comparisons that Sales can use in active deals.

Migration playbooks by persona and stack

How does a Marketing org transition from Adobe? How does an HR team move comms work in-house? How does a Sales team replace agency-built decks? Each playbook anchored by a customer story.

Partner co-marketing for technology consolidation

Joint events with Salesforce, Microsoft, Workday on integrating Canva into existing workflows. The message: Canva does not replace your platform of record — it enhances it.

Customer reference program for offboarding

Connect prospects with customers who have made the same transition. Make the path visible.

Procurement-ready content

TCO calculators, migration cost estimates, security checklists, SOC 2 reports. Everything Procurement needs to greenlight a deal in one place.

Estimated impact: this pillar alone could compress enterprise sales cycles by 30%+ by removing the "but how?" friction.

07 · A flagship program

Create Raving Fans.

Make every B2B customer an organised advocate. Make customer advocacy the primary demand engine for enterprise.

Why this is the most leveraged investment in the strategy.

Customer reference programs in B2B are usually defensive — "we need a reference for this deal." That treats customers as a fulfillment resource. Canva has something most B2B companies do not: an installed base of customers who already love the product. 95% of Fortune 500 are customers. 149% net dollar retention. The asset is unprecedented. The program design needs to match the scale of the asset.

Create Raving Fans is built to do exactly that — turn every Canva customer into an organised, activated advocate, and turn customer advocacy into the engine that powers every other pillar in this strategy. References accelerate Pipeline Generation. Champion stories drive PLG-to-Enterprise Conversion. Pioneer customers fuel Credibility. Peer outreach scales Regional Execution. Customer data feeds Measurement. Done well, Create Raving Fans is the multiplier on every other investment.

Six components.

01 · The Pioneer Program

The top tier. Twelve to fifteen strategic enterprise customers selected annually. They become Canva's design partners — not just customers.

What they get. Early access to AI 2.0 features and roadmap. Direct line to product leadership. Executive sponsorship from a Canva executive (CMO, CTO, CRO). Custom AI 2.0 use case development with the Canva creative team. Annual Pioneer Summit — intimate, executive-level. Co-marketing investment.

What Canva gets. Twelve to fifteen deeply committed flagship customers. Premium case studies and video stories. Speaking slots at Canva Create Enterprise and tentpole events. Named references for the highest-stakes deals. Roadmap validation. Peer outreach to top-priority prospects.

02 · The Champion Network

The mid tier. Two hundred-plus champions across the customer base — the Brand Architects and Force Multipliers from the persona framework, identified, organised, activated.

What they get. Quarterly virtual peer roundtables. Annual in-person Champion Summit. Recognition program — Canva Champion badge. Early product feedback loops. Champion-only community (Slack or Circle). Free Canva Pro for personal use as recognition.

What Canva gets. An activated network for peer-to-peer prospecting. Deep, current usage stories. Internal champions actively pitching Canva up the organisation chart in their own companies.

03 · Peer-to-peer prospecting

The motion. Operationalised customer-to-prospect introductions, built into the Sales workflow.

How it works. Sales rep flags a prospect → matched in under twenty-four hours to a customer in the same persona, vertical, and stage. Thirty-minute calls, agenda set, customer pre-briefed. Customer "open office hours" — recurring slots where any prospect can join and ask peers anything.

Compensation. Recognition and exclusive access — never money. Paying for references corrupts the integrity of the program. The currency is status, access, and peer community.

Tracked. Reference-attributed deal velocity acceleration. Reference-to-close rate. Customer participation rate.

04 · The Reference Engine

The infrastructure. Software-driven matching. Every customer indexed by persona match (Force Multiplier, Brand Architect, Mandate Carrier, Risk Closer, Commercial Pragmatist), industry vertical, use case, deployment stage, reference availability, and language capability.

The standard. When Sales needs a reference, they get a precision-matched customer in under twenty-four hours. Industry standard for reference programs is "we don't know — let me check." Canva turns this into a service-level commitment.

The stack. ReferenceEdge or Influitive integrated with Salesforce. Reference activity logged against opportunities for closed-loop attribution.

05 · Storytelling at scale

The library. One hundred-plus customer stories per year, indexed by persona × industry × use case. Every story produced in three formats so it works across every channel.

  • Thirty-second video — for ABM and paid social
  • Ninety-second video — for events and Sales enablement
  • Long-form written case study — for procurement and analyst evidence

The story types. The migration story (offboarding from Adobe). The transformation story (AI-native rollout). The governance story (brand control at scale). The consolidation story (vendor rationalisation). The pioneer story (early adopter wins).

06 · Canva Customer Awards

The annual program. Awards mapped one-to-one to the five personas — so every winner has a clear story shape and a clear audience to inspire.

  • The Force Multiplier Award — best content engine
  • The Brand Architect Award — best brand governance at scale
  • The Mandate Carrier Award — best AI-native transformation
  • The Risk Closer Award — best governance and AI deployment
  • The Commercial Pragmatist Award — best vendor consolidation outcome

Amplification. Announced at Canva Create Enterprise. Winners amplified across the year — social, PR, customer videos, speaking slots, ABM creative. Aspiration created in the customer base. Social proof manufactured at scale.

Operating mechanics.

  • Leadership. A dedicated Customer Marketing leader reporting to Field Marketing.
  • Team. Four to five people: Pioneer Program manager, Champion Network manager, Reference Engine operations, Storytelling and content, Events and awards.
  • Sales partnership. Weekly review on which references closed which deals — reference-to-revenue attribution treated like pipeline-to-revenue attribution.
  • Tech stack. ReferenceEdge or Influitive integrated with Salesforce, content stored and indexed in a customer story library accessible to Sales and Marketing.
  • KPIs. References-per-month delivered. Reference-to-close rate. Peer-call deal acceleration. NPS lift among enterprise base. Expansion revenue attributable to advocacy.
Most companies treat customer reference as a fulfillment function. Canva should treat it as the primary demand engine for enterprise — because the asset already exists. Every Canva customer is already a fan. 95% of Fortune 500 are customers. The only thing missing is the program design that matches the scale of the asset.
08 · Visual experiences

Programs must reflect the brand.

Canva's brand is creative, design-forward, and distinctive. Enterprise field programs must reflect that. Standard CXO dinners and beige booths fail the brand and underwhelm the buyer.

Canva Studios at tentpole events

Instead of standard booths at SaaStr or Dreamforce, build creation studios — live design experiences where attendees see Canva in action with celebrity designers, on-the-spot rebranding sessions for attendees, AI-assisted creation demos. The booth itself becomes a marketing moment.

Immersive executive experiences

Replace the standard CXO dinner with curated creative experiences — private gallery openings, design studio tours, sessions with Canva's in-house creative team. The point is to give executives a memorable encounter with the brand, not a steakhouse.

Canva Create for Enterprise

A dedicated enterprise track at the existing Canva Create event, or a separate annual Enterprise Summit. Customer-led content, deep product roadmap, and immersive brand experiences.

Visual thought leadership

Reports like "State of Visual Communication" delivered as visual artifacts, not PDFs. Interactive microsites, designed publications, video-first storytelling. The medium reinforces the message.

This is not aesthetic — it is strategic. Canva's competitive moat is design. Field programs that look like everyone else's actively undermine the positioning.
09 · Distinct identity

Building Canva Enterprise as its own thing.

Inside Canva, Enterprise sits in the shadow of Education and Consumer. Both have clearer narratives, bigger stages, and stronger internal momentum. Field marketing has a role to play in correcting that — not by fighting for resources, but by giving Enterprise its own identity.

Enterprise narrative ownership

Working with Product Marketing to establish the Creative OS, AI workflow, and brand governance positioning as the distinct enterprise story — separate from "Canva for Schools" and "Canva for everyone."

Enterprise customer community

A formal customer advisory board, peer roundtables, and a customer-led content engine — operated as part of the Create Raving Fans program. Enterprise customers should know each other and amplify each other.

Enterprise visibility internally

Quarterly enterprise wins playbacks to the broader Canva team. Customer stories that show up in internal comms and external content. Make enterprise wins as visible as education and consumer milestones.

Enterprise voice externally

Dedicated enterprise content, dedicated enterprise events, dedicated enterprise customer advocates. The market should know Canva has a serious enterprise business — not just deduce it from logos.
