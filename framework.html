Part one

The integrated field marketing framework

A company-agnostic framework for building B2B field marketing functions that generate measurable pipeline. Developed across 15 years building field marketing from zero at five companies across global enterprise markets.

01 — The problem
Most B2B marketing organisations are still operating on a model designed for a different era

B2B marketing has fundamentally changed over the past 15 years, but most organisations haven't updated their operating model. They're running demand generation playbooks built for a world where buyers followed a linear funnel, sales controlled information, and marketing's job was to fill the top. That world no longer exists.

Over-indexing on demand capture
Most B2B teams pour budget into capturing existing intent (paid search, trade shows, gated content) while doing almost nothing to create new demand. They're fishing in a shrinking pond.
Marketing and Sales misalignment
Marketing measures MQLs. Sales measures revenue. Neither owns the pipeline in between. Field programs get planned in isolation from deal cycles.
Attribution theatre
Last-touch attribution rewards capture programs and makes creation programs invisible. Marketing cuts the activities that build future pipeline because they can't "prove" they worked in 30 days.
One-size-fits-all programs
The same webinar gets sent to a CIO and a marketing coordinator. Different buyers at different stages need fundamentally different programs.
02 — How B2B marketing has changed
Three eras of B2B go-to-market
2008–2015 · The lead gen era
Fill the funnel, let Sales sort it out
Marketing existed to generate volume. Success was measured by MQL count.
  • Buyers depended on sales reps for information
  • Linear funnel: awareness → interest → decision
  • Marketing measured by volume (MQLs)
  • Events = lead capture machines
2015–2021 · The ABM era
Target the right accounts, align with Sales
ABM replaced spray-and-pray. But it was still fundamentally a capture motion.
  • Buyers self-educated before talking to sales
  • Account-based replaced lead-based
  • Marketing measured by pipeline influence
  • Events = relationship accelerators
2021–now · The creation era
Build the market, then capture it
The best companies realised most of their TAM isn't in-market at any given time.
  • Buyers complete 70%+ of journey before engaging sales
  • Dark social and peer influence drive decisions
  • Marketing measured by pipeline sourced + revenue
  • Events = category-defining experiences

The thesis: Field marketing is the only function that can operate across both demand creation and demand capture — because it sits at the intersection of Sales, Product Marketing, and the customer. But to do this effectively, you need an integrated framework that connects buyer awareness, program design, account segmentation, persona targeting, and measurement into a single system.

03 — Buyer awareness stages
Where accounts sit determines which motion to run

Adapted from Eugene Schwartz's awareness model. Demand creation moves buyers through the left three stages. Demand capture converts the right two.

Unaware
"We outsource all this — it's not our problem"
Problem aware
"This is getting expensive and slow"
Solution aware
"We need to bring this in-house"
Product aware
"This product could solve it"
Most aware
"We need to buy this"

04 — Demand creation × demand capture
Two fundamentally different motions, different tactics, different measurement

Inspired by Chris Walker's framework at Refine Labs. These are not two ends of a spectrum — they are two different jobs with different timelines and different ways of proving impact.

Demand creation

Change what buyers believe. Create future pipeline by shaping how the market thinks — before they enter a buying cycle.
Executive engagement
Intimate experiences that reposition the brand with senior decision-makers
Thought leadership
Analyst partnerships and co-authored research that builds enterprise credibility
Customer storytelling
Peer-led events that create social proof through authentic customer voices
Category-defining events
Owned events that establish the company as the category leader

Demand capture

Convert existing intent. Intercept buyers already in-market, already evaluating, or already using the product.
Account-based marketing
Multi-touch campaigns targeting named accounts with personalised outreach
Industry events and trade shows
Sponsorships with speaking slots, demos, and executive meetings
Partner co-marketing
Joint campaigns with strategic partners to capture referred demand
Customer reference programs
Connecting prospects with customers to validate and accelerate procurement
05 — ICP tiering
Account segmentation determines field investment level
Tier 1 — Strategic
Largest, highest-value accounts
Complex buying committees, long sales cycles. Justify dedicated field resources.
1:1 · Named account plans · Executive engagement · Custom programs
Tier 2 — Growth
Fast-scaling mid-enterprise
Ready for enterprise solutions but need education and validation.
1:few · Regional roadshows · Industry roundtables · Vertical campaigns
Tier 3 — Commercial
High-velocity mid-market
Shorter sales cycles, high volume. Field supports scaled and digital motions.
1:many · Virtual workshops · Webinars · Self-serve acceleration
06 — Buying committee personas
Different personas require different motions and different measurement
Capture
Champion
Functional user / team lead
Already uses the product. Needs enterprise features to make the case internally.
Creation
Gatekeeper
CIO / CISO
Doesn't know the product is enterprise-grade. Needs security and compliance validation.
Creation → capture
Business buyer
CMO / VP
Knows the product in a consumer context. Needs repositioning to enterprise value.
Creation
Line of business head
Dept VPs (Sales, HR, Comms)
Outsources creative to agencies. Wants to in-house but doesn't yet see the product as the answer.
Capture
Rationaliser
Procurement
Consolidating SaaS spend. Needs TCO comparison and analyst validation.
The rule: Executive dinners reach gatekeepers and line of business heads (creation). Workshops and demos reach champions and rationalisers (capture). A field program portfolio must deliberately cover the full buying committee.
07 — The operating system
Three mechanisms that connect the framework into a live system
Pipeline council with Sales
Weekly reviews to align field programs with active pipeline, account stage, and persona engagement.
Product data → field signals
Usage data triggers the right field program for the right account at the right awareness stage.
Closed-loop attribution
Self-reported for creation, software-attributed for capture — each motion measured on its own terms.
08 — Measurement by motion
The single biggest mistake: measuring creation and capture the same way
Demand creation
  • Brand perception shift among target accounts
  • Executive engagement depth and frequency
  • Analyst coverage and category positioning
  • Self-reported attribution
  • Approved vendor list additions (long-cycle)
Demand capture
  • Pipeline generated and influenced
  • Event-to-SQO conversion rate
  • Cost-per-opportunity by program type
  • Software-attributed pipeline and revenue
  • Deal velocity acceleration from field touches
How the framework connects: The buyer awareness model tells you where an account sits. Demand creation and capture tell you which motion to run. ICP tiers determine investment level. Persona mapping determines which programs reach which members of the buying committee. Product data is the connective tissue. And the measurement system ensures you're evaluating each motion on its own terms.
